Bio

Over the past few years I have been working towards fulfilling my ambition to become a software engineer. During my first few years at Stony Brook, I had no idea what my passion was. After bouncing around from major to major I settled on chemistry because I enjoyed understanding the tiny details of the world around us.

After working as a chemist for a few years, I realized that was certainly not what I wanted to do for the rest of my life. I eventually came across the wonderful world of programming by taking a few introductory courses online. I was drawn to the freedom of creativity, open ended problem solving, and the satisfaction of overcoming challenges.

I returned to school to obtain my second B.S. in Computer Science from Oregon State University. During this time, I continued to work as a chemist until I received an internship as a software engineer at PSEG Long Island. After graduation I began my employment at Northwell Health's Feinstein Institutes for Medical Research. Over the past two years I developed software not only for medical research, but general hospital operations, quality improvement, data manipulation and analysis. Aside from this, I overhauled several archaic systems and formed a more modern architecture for years to come.

Interests

At one of my first positions, a coworker asked me, “So, what’s your story?” I was at a loss for words. How do I sum up my entire life story in a few words? I often think back to this and attempt to imagine what the best answer to that question could have been. Quite frankly, I still don’t have an answer. I don’t want to look at old pages in my story, I want to make and experience new ones.

Similarly, I enjoy creative writing and often practice my writing skills with various prompts. I’m inspired by the philosophical fiction works of Albert Camus. I hope to one day publish a novel that is at the very least worth leaving a bad review on Goodreads.

When I’m not being inspired by Camus, I’m following Gordon Ramsay’s instructions in the kitchen through his cookbook, “Home Cooking”. I really believe my cooking skills have improved immensely and I would make Ramsay proud (my wife believes otherwise…).

My story is simple. I’m a programmer and developer by day; looking up solutions on Stack Overflow and learning and creating new mobile apps. By night, I’m a Ramsay prodigy, bookworm, and cat whisperer (I’ve been trying to teach my Russian Blue how to fetch).
